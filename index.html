Preprints and Publications

  1. Fine Structure of Rupture Set for Semilinear Elliptic Equation with Singular Nonlinearity
    Wei Wang and Zhifei Zhang. arXiv preprint arXiv:2411.16048. arXiv.
    In this paper, we study the stationary solutions of semilinear elliptic equation with singular nonlinearity $$ \Delta u=u^{-p}+f,\,\,u\geq 0\quad\text{in }\Omega\subset\mathbb{R}^n, $$ where \( n\geq 2 \), \( p>1 \), \( \Omega \) is a bounded domain, and \( f\in L^q(\Omega) \) with \( \frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2p}<\frac{q}{n} \). We establish a sharp estimate for the Minkowski content of the rupture set \( \{u=0\} \) and demonstrate that this set is \( (n-2) \)-rectifiable. For this, we examine the stratification of the rupture set based on the symmetry properties of tangent functions, leading to the proof of \( k \)-rectifiability for each \( k \)-stratum. As a significant byproduct of our analysis, we improve the integrability of \( D^ju \) with \( j\in\mathbb{Z}_+ \) to the optimal Lorentz space \( L^{\frac{2(p+1)}{j(p+1)-2},\infty} \), under the assumption that \( D^{j-1}f \) is bounded. As an application of our results in the static case of the equation, for a class of suitable weak solutions to the three-dimensional evolutional problem $$ \partial_tu=\Delta u-u^{-p},\,\,u\geq 0\quad\text{in }(\Omega\subset\mathbb{R}^3)\times(0,T), $$ where \( p>3 \) and \( T>0 \), we show that \( \{u(\cdot,t)=0\} \) is \( 1 \)-rectifiable for a.e. \( t\in(0,T) \).
  2. Quantitative Stratification and Sharp Regularity Estimates for Supercritical Semilinear Elliptic Equations
    Haotong Fu, Wei Wang, and Zhifei Zhang. arXiv preprint arXiv:2408.06726. arXiv.
    In this paper, we investigate the interior regularity theory for stationary solutions of the supercritical semilinear elliptic equation $$ -\Delta u=|u|^{p-1}u\quad\text{in }\Omega,\quad p>\frac{n+2}{n-2}, $$ where \( \Omega\subset\mathbb{R}^n \) is a bounded domain with \( n\geq 3 \). Our primary focus is on structures of the stratification for the singular sets. We define the \( k \)-th stratification \( S^k(u) \) of \( u \) based on the tangent functions and tangent measures. We show that \( S^k(u) \) is \( k \)-rectifiable and establish estimates for volumes associated with points, which have lower bounds on the regular scales. These estimates enable us to derive sharp interior estimates for the solutions. Specifically, if \( \alpha_p=\frac{2(p+1)}{p-1} \) is not an integer, then for any \( j\in\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0} \), we have $$ D^ju\in L_{\text{loc}}^{q_j,\infty}(\Omega). $$ Precisely, for any \( \Omega'\subset\subset\Omega \), $$ \sup\{\lambda>0:\lambda^{q_j}\mathcal{L}^n(\{x\in\Omega':|D^ju(x)|>\lambda\})\}<+\infty, $$ where \( \mathcal{L}^n(\cdot) \) is the \( n \)-dimensional Lebesgue measure, and $$ q_j=\frac{(p-1)(\lfloor\alpha_p\rfloor+1)}{2+j(p-1)}, $$ with \( \lfloor\alpha_p\rfloor \) being the integer part of \( \alpha_p \). The proofs of these results rely on Reifenberg-type theorems developed by A. Naber and D. Valtorta to study the stratification of harmonic maps.
  3. Landau-de Gennes Model with Sextic Potentials: Asymptotic Behavior of Minimizers
    Wei Wang and Zhifei Zhang. arXiv preprint arXiv:2404.00677. arXiv.
    We study a class of Landau-de Gennes energy functionals with a sextic bulk energy density in a three-dimensional domain. We examine the asymptotic behavior of uniformly bounded minimizers in two distinct scenarios: one where their energy remains uniformly bounded and another where it logarithmically diverges as a function of the elastic constant. In the first case, we show that up to a subsequence, the minimizers converge to a locally minimizing harmonic map in both the \( H_{\text{loc}}^1 \) and \( C_{\text{loc}}^j, j\in \mathbb{Z}_+ \) norms within compact subsets that are distant from the singularities of the limit. For the second case, we establish the existence of a closed set denoted as \( \mathcal{S}_{\text{line}} \). This set has a finite length and consists of finite segments of lines locally such that the energy of minimizers is locally uniformly bounded away from it. This work solves an open question raised by Canevari (ARMA, 223 (2017), 591-676) concerning point and line defects in the Landau-de Gennes model with sextic potentials.

  5. Uniform Estimates of Resolvents in Homogenization Theory of Elliptic Systems
    Journal of Differential Equations, 370 (2023): 1-65. journal arXiv.
    In this paper, we study the estimates of resolvents \( R(\lambda,\mathcal{L}_{\varepsilon})=(\mathcal{L}_{\varepsilon}-\lambda I)^{-1} \), where $$ \mathcal{L}_{\varepsilon}=-\operatorname{div}(A(x/\varepsilon)\nabla) $$ is a family of second elliptic operators with symmetric, periodic, and oscillating coefficients defined on a bounded domain \( \Omega \) with \( \varepsilon>0 \). For \( p\in(1,+\infty) \), we will establish uniform \( L^p\to L^p \), \( L^p\to W_0^{1,p} \), \( W^{-1,p}\to L^p \) and \( W^{-1,p}\to W_0^{1,p} \) estimates by using the real variable method. Meanwhile, we use Green functions for operators \( \mathcal{L}_{\varepsilon}-\lambda I \) to study the asymptotic behavior of \( R(\lambda,\mathcal{L}_{\varepsilon}) \) and obtain convergence estimates in \( L^p\to L^p \), \( L^p\to W_0^{1,p} \) norm.
  6. Homogenization Theory of Elliptic System with Lower Order Terms for Dimension Two
    Wei Wang and Ting Zhang. Communications on Pure and Applied Analysis, 22.3 (2023): 787-824. journal arXiv.
    In this paper, we consider the homogenization problem for generalized elliptic systems $$ \mathcal{L}_{\varepsilon}=-\operatorname{div}(A(x/\varepsilon)\nabla+V(x/\varepsilon))+B(x/\varepsilon)\nabla+c(x/\varepsilon)+\lambda I $$ with dimension two. Precisely, we will establish the \( W^{1,p} \) estimates, Hölder estimates, Lipschitz estimates and \( L^p \) convergence results for \( \mathcal{L}_{\varepsilon} \) with dimension two. The operator \( \mathcal{L}_{\varepsilon} \) has been studied by Qiang Xu with dimension \( d\geq 3 \) in (JMAA, 438 (2016), 1574-1600 & JDE, 261 (2016), 4368-4423) and the case \( d=2 \) remains unsolved. As a byproduct, we will construct the Green functions for \( \mathcal{L}_{\varepsilon} \) with \( d=2 \) and their convergence rates.

Notes and Explanatory Papers

  • A Review of the Lin-Wang Bubble Analysis in the Study of the Generalized Ginzburg-Landau Model. pdf.
    In the reading report, we concisely review the blow-up result for the generalized static Ginzburg-Landau model discussed in the renowned paper (CAG, 7 (1999), 397-429). We employ a straightforward argument from (Ann. of Math. (2), 149 (1999), 785-829) to modify the proof of the main theorem presented in the paper by Lin and Wang. The generalized Ginzburg-Landau model is a nonlinear variational model with several specialized structures. These properties enable us to conduct more explicit analysis and apply geometric measure theory, which differs from the primarily topological methods introduced in the course "Nonlinear Analysis" this semester.
  • Studying Notes of the Ergodic Theory (in Chinese). pdf.
    This note is from the course "Ergodic Theory" delivered by Prof. Shaobo Gan.
  • Reading Notes of a Short Proof of the Schoen-Uhlenbeck Partial Regularity Theory for Harmonic Maps. pdf.
    Harmonic maps are nonlinear extensions of harmonic functions. They are critical points of Dirichlet energy functionals between Riemannian manifolds. There are abundant materials in the study of harmonic maps. (JDG, 17 (1982), 307-335) by Schoen and Uhlenbeck is one of the most important works on harmonic maps. However, the proofs in this paper are complicated and technical and are not easy to read. Recently, in (SCM, 47 (2017), 1199-1206), Lin gave brief proofs for the main results by Schoen and Uhlenbeck. Due to limited spaces, the paper by Lin's lacks of details. In this paper, we will complete the details and review some classical and recent results in harmonic maps.
  • Reading Notes of "Lectures on Elliptic Free Boundary Problems" by Fanghua Lin. pdf.
    Elliptic equations with free boundary is of great importance in mathematics and physics. There is a famous lecture note by Prof. Fanghua Lin on this topic, named "Lectures on Elliptic Free Boundary Problems." This lecture note is rich in content and is an introductory reading material for learning the free boundary theory of elliptic equations. Because this note covers many aspects of theory and is limited in length, there are many omissions in detail. In the Fall 2022, we studied this lecture note in a seminar at Peking University. We have supplemented the proof details for most of this note.
